Success Spotlight #1

A Real-World Win from Anna

The Problem: Anna was starting a search for a "Head of Digital Marketing" in a competitive fintech market. The old way involved hours of manual research on LinkedIn to understand the landscape.

The New Way: She built one powerful prompt to do the work for her—structured using C.R.E.A.T.E.

Anna's Winning "Talent Landscape Snapshot" Prompt (C.R.E.A.T.E.)

[CHARACTER] You are a senior market intelligence analyst for a top executive search firm, specializing in the European fintech sector.
[REQUEST] Create a "Talent Landscape Snapshot" for a "Head of Digital Marketing" role for a fast-growing fintech startup in Budapest.
[EXAMPLES] Provide headings and short example bullets under each heading (e.g., how a target company line might look).
[ADDITIONS] Include immediate market context: competitive firms, seniority bands, and common alternative job titles in the market.
[TYPE] Output: Headed sections — "Target Companies", "Common Job Titles", "Key Skills & Technologies". Keep lists concise (2–5 items each).
[EXTRAS] Tone: factual and recruiter-facing; prioritize clarity; do not include recruitment slogans.

The Impact: From Hours to Seconds

  • The AI returned a perfectly formatted snapshot in ~30 seconds.
  • It identified target companies and more precise job titles like "VP of Growth."
  • It listed key technologies such as "HubSpot" and "Salesforce Marketing Cloud."
  • This saved at least 1 hour of manual research and gave her a huge confidence boost.

Clinic Case Study: Peter's Problem

The Challenge: Peter wanted help writing screening questions for a "Junior Accountant" role, but his results were too generic.

Peter's "Before" Prompt:

Give me interview questions for a junior accountant.

The Group's "After" Prompt (C.R.E.A.T.E.):

[CHARACTER] You are a hiring manager and structured-interview specialist.
[REQUEST] Generate screening and interview questions for a "Junior Accountant" role.
[EXAMPLES] Show 2–3 sample behavioral questions for each category to demonstrate format (e.g., "Describe a time when...").
[ADDITIONS] Key qualifications: attention to detail, Excel pivot tables, willingness to learn; role is entry-level with basic accounting tasks.
[TYPE] Organize questions into categories: "Attention to Detail", "Excel Skills", "Learning Agility". Provide 4–6 questions per category.
[EXTRAS] Tone: professional and neutral; include interviewer guidance for scoring (one-sentence rubric per question).

The Result: From Generic to Perfectly Structured

By adding Character, Additions (context), Type (format), and Examples, the AI produced specific behavioral and technical questions organized into the exact categories needed for a structured screening call. Peter got a ready-to-use list in minutes.
