Success Spotlight #1

A Real-World Win from Anna

The Problem: Anna was starting a search for a "Head of Digital Marketing" in a competitive fintech market. The old way involved hours of manual research on LinkedIn to understand the landscape.

The New Way: She built one powerful C.R.E.A.T.E. prompt to do the work for her.

Anna's Winning "Talent Landscape Snapshot" Prompt (C.R.E.A.T.E.):

[CHARACTER] You are a senior market intelligence analyst for a top executive search firm, specializing in the European fintech sector.
[REQUEST] I am starting a search for a "Head of Digital Marketing" for a fast-growing fintech startup in Budapest. Produce a Talent Landscape Snapshot to quickly guide sourcing and outreach.
[EXAMPLES] Show the output structure and sample bullets for one target company and one job title example so I can see the desired format.
[ADDITIONS] Include the top 5 competing companies (brief rationale), the 2–3 most common related job titles, and a list of 10–15 key skills & technologies commonly required.
[TYPE] Output headings: "Target Companies", "Common Job Titles", "Key Skills & Technologies". Use short bullets under each heading.
[EXTRAS] Tone: concise and analytical; length: one-page summary; highlight any surprising gaps or opportunities.

The Impact: From Hours to Seconds

  • The AI produced a perfectly formatted report in ~30 seconds.
  • It identified target companies and more specific job titles like "VP of Growth."
  • It listed key technologies like "HubSpot" and "Salesforce Marketing Cloud."
  • This saved at least 1 hour of manual research and gave Anna more confidence to engage with hiring managers.

Live Prompt Clinic #1

Your Turn to Get Help

The Rule: This is a "no-judgment zone." If you're stuck, chances are someone else is too. We will fix prompts as a group using the C.R.E.A.T.E. structure.

Clinic Case Study: Peter's Problem

The Challenge: Peter wanted help writing screening questions for a "Junior Accountant" role, but his results were too generic.

Peter's "Before" Prompt:

Give me interview questions for a junior accountant.

The Group's "After" Prompt (C.R.E.A.T.E.):

[CHARACTER] You are an experienced hiring manager and interviewer for finance roles.
[REQUEST] Create screening and behavioral interview questions for a "Junior Accountant" candidate.
[EXAMPLES] Provide an example behavioral question and model answer (short) for "Attention to Detail".
[ADDITIONS] Key qualifications: attention to detail, Excel pivot tables, eagerness to learn; role is entry-level with some monthly reporting duties.
[TYPE] Organize questions into categories: "Attention to Detail", "Excel Skills", "Learning Agility" — 4–6 questions per category.
[EXTRAS] Tone: professional and practical; include 1–2 red flags to watch for in candidate answers.

The Result: From Generic to Perfectly Structured

By adding Character, Context (Additions), and Format (Type) along with an Example, the AI produced specific, behavioral questions organized into the exact categories needed for a structured screening call. Peter received a ready-to-use set of questions in minutes.
