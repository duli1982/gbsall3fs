Session 2.1: AI for Advanced Sourcing

Our Goal: Use AI to move beyond obvious keywords and become true market intelligence experts. We will find better candidates, faster.

Part A: Boolean & Beyond

Stop Brainstorming Keywords. Start Generating Them.

The old way of writing a Boolean string is slow: you try to think of every synonym and title. The new way is to tell the AI exactly what you need using a structured prompt and let it do the heavy lifting.

The Evolution of a Search Prompt

  1. Start with a simple idea: "Software Engineer with Python."
  2. Ask AI to expand it: "Generate a list of 20 alternative job titles and 30 related technical skills."
  3. Ask AI to build it: "Using the titles and skills, build a complex Boolean search string for LinkedIn."
View the "Boolean Builder" Prompt Example (C.R.E.A.T.E.)
[CHARACTER] You are an expert tech sourcer with deep knowledge of LinkedIn Recruiter's search syntax.

[REQUEST] I need a comprehensive search string to find a "Senior Software Engineer" who uses Python, AWS, and database technologies in the Budapest area.

[EXAMPLES] First provide a brainstorm list of 15 alternative job titles and 20 related technical skills (example format: "Title — short note"). Then show how those terms map into boolean groups.

[ADDITIONS] Prioritize seniority, exclude junior roles, include location filters for Budapest, and prefer profiles that list "AWS" and "Postgres" explicitly.

[TYPE] Output: 1) Brainstorm lists, 2) Grouped boolean term sets, 3) Final LinkedIn-optimized Boolean string in a code block.

[EXTRAS] Keep the boolean string compatible with LinkedIn Recruiter; include comments explaining each group; avoid overly long OR lists that reduce precision.

Part B: AI as Your Research Assistant

Answer Questions in Minutes, Not Hours.

Sourcing isn't just about finding people; it's about understanding the market. AI can be your personal research analyst. Here are three key research prompts reworked to follow C.R.E.A.T.E.

1. Market Mapping

"List the top 10 biotech companies specializing in gene therapy in the Boston area."

2. Competitor Analysis

"Who are the main competitors for Company X, and what are their typical software engineering job titles?"

3. Rapid CV Analysis

"Summarize this CV and create a table comparing the candidate's experience against these 5 key requirements from the job description."

View the CV Summarization Prompt Example (C.R.E.A.T.E.)
[CHARACTER] You are a senior RPO recruiter tasked with quickly screening candidates against a job description.

[REQUEST] Read the CV and the Job Description provided below and produce an objective match analysis.

[EXAMPLES] Provide a sample row for the table as an example: Key Requirement — Evidence from CV — Match Level (High/Medium/Low).

[ADDITIONS] Focus on the five listed key requirements; highlight missing or strong evidence items; ignore irrelevant experience older than 10 years unless directly applicable.

[TYPE] Output: ONLY a table with columns "Key Requirement", "Evidence from CV", "Match Level (High/Medium/Low)".

[EXTRAS] Keep results concise; do not add commentary outside the table; flag any severe gaps with "Low (critical)".

Lab Activities

Lab 1: The Boolean Builder (20 Mins)

Your Goal: Create a comprehensive Boolean string for a niche, hard-to-fill role like "Clinical Research Associate with Oncology experience."

  • Work in your breakout groups.
  • Use the C.R.E.A.T.E. structure to write a prompt that produces title & skill brainstorms, then a boolean string.
  • Run the prompt in Gemini and iterate.
  • Crucially: Test the resulting Boolean string in a real LinkedIn search!

Lab 2: The 5-Minute Market Map (20 Mins)

Your Goal: Quickly gather actionable intelligence on a specific market to inform your sourcing strategy.

  • Choose a role and a city.
  • Use research prompts built with C.R.E.A.T.E. to produce a one-page "Market Snapshot."
  • Your snapshot must include: top 5 target companies, 3–5 common job titles, and a summary of one sample profile.

Key Takeaways & Next Steps

Your new superpower is letting AI do the heavy lifting on Search Logic, Market Research, and Candidate Analysis. Use the C.R.E.A.T.E. framework to structure every sourcing/research prompt so outputs are reliable and repeatable.
